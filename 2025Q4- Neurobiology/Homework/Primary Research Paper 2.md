### Comprehension Questions

**What was the primary objective of the study conducted by Gazzaniga, Bogen, and Sperry in this article?** To study the effects of severing the commissures on the communication between the hemispheres, as well as the ability of each hemisphere to operate independently, and most importantly in the context of a human patient.

**What are the cerebral commissures, and what role do they play in brain function?** Nerve fiber bundles that transmit information between the left and right hemispheres.

**Which specific commissures were sectioned in the patient studied in this research?** The corpus callosum, as well as the anterior and hippocampal commissures.

**How did the researchers test visual processing in each hemisphere?** By presenting a card briefly in either the right or left visual field while the patient focused on a center point, so as to isolate the hemisphere being tested.


### Analysis and Interpretation Questions

**Describe the key differences in verbal responses when stimuli were presented to the right versus the left visual field. What do these differences reveal about hemispheric specialization?** Right visual field stimuli had little difficulty being named, left visual field stimuli auditory processing was pretty much completely destroyed. This shows that language (I called it auditory processing although that's probably incorrect) is processed in the left hemisphere.

**How did the findings contribute to our understanding of lateralization of language in the human brain?** The findings showed that without the commissures, the right hemisphere cannot communicate information to the left hemisphere to be processed into speech, at least quickly.

**Why was it important that the stimuli were briefly flashed to one visual field? What would be the consequence of longer exposure?** To ensure that stimuli were being processed _only_ by the hemisphere being tested; with longer exposure the eyes could move to the stimuli and compromise the isolation.

**What were the implications of the patient's ability to draw or select objects with the left hand that they could not verbally identify?** That the right hemisphere possesses cognitive abilities independent of the left hemisphere but that verbal processing is completely dependent on those commissures.
