We learn about the regression and classification models of supervised learning. Regression involves training a model to predict an output that is numerical in some way. The book uses features of a house to predict the price of it (virtually useless outside of a vacuum, but we roll with it). Classification is... kind of the inverse? It uses different flavors of technique to predict the likelihood of behavior, like using different factors to determine if a customer will buy a product.
