Alexis Baker


Databases


Reading 1 (Zybook 16-17)


Chapter 16 covers the basics of database design and construction. It goes over what databases are, what they can be used for, and how basic elements of them work. A lot of these terms are very familiar if you've ever worked with spreadsheets. There was also considerable amounts of crossover with my hobbies; I do statistic tracking for a couple of sports and I have an interest in elections, both of which involve considerable use of databases.


Chapter 17 covers, well, a lot. It goes over different types of database models and their different "essential particle", as in what they use as the fundamental building block for organization, as well as introducing us, formally, to SQL, the language (or rather, language family) used to write them. We learn the most important aspects of SQL and how to implement them properly, as well as how **not** to implement them.
