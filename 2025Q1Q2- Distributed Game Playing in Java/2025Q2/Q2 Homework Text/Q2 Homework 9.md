### Part 2

**Question 1**

We implemented a kamikaze function that directs some baby rats to attack the cats at all costs, which can create an opportunity for more points, as well as low-cost, high-reward pathfinding that allowed our rats to search for cheese and enemies more effectively.

**Question 2**

In Helldivers 2, you work with a team to complete a major objective. The game strongly disincentivizes being the opposite of a team player; rewards are collective, as are points earned from completing objectives. Teamwork and communication are absolutely essential to success, which is why I pretty much only play the game with friends, and there's a very strong feeling of accomplishment that comes from effective teamwork. The game is also extremely difficult at the levels where you want certain rewards to be found; the stress from these objectives is higher than any game I've ever played, leaving me feeling exhausted, physically, at the end of it.
