**Explain BFS for a graph.**

Explores the graph level by level, visiting all neighbors of a vertex before moving on.


**Explain DFS for a graph.**

Explores the graph as deep as possible, following one unvisited adjacent vertex until it comes across one that it has visited, at which point it backtracks.


**What is the difference between DFS and DFS2 in the text?**

DFS is meant to compute the time to complete of the original graph, and DFS2 is meant to identify strong edges between vertices in a tranposed graph.
